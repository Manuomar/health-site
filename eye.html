Eye care refers to the practices and measures taken to maintain the health and well-being 
of the eyes. Good eye care is essential for maintaining proper vision and preventing eye 
problems and diseases. Here are some important aspects of eye care:

Regular Eye Exams

Routine eye exams are crucial for maintaining optimal eye health. An eye care professional,
such as an optometrist or ophthalmologist, can detect any vision problems, check for eye 
diseases, and prescribe corrective lenses if necessary.

Proper Vision Correction

If you have refractive errors like nearsightedness, farsightedness, or astigmatism, 
it's important to wear corrective lenses, such as glasses or contact lenses, 
as prescribed by your eye care professional. Using the correct prescription helps 
alleviate eye strain and ensures clear vision.

Protecting Your Eyes

Shielding your eyes from potential hazards is essential. When engaging in activities 
like sports, DIY projects, or working with chemicals or tools, wear protective eyewear 
such as safety glasses or goggles to prevent eye injuries.

Resting Your Eyes

Extended periods of screen time or focusing on a particular task can strain your eyes.
Follow the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes, take a 20-second break, and look at something 
20 feet away. This practice helps reduce eye strain and fatigue.

Healthy Lifestyle Choices

A balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and omega-3 fatty acids can promote eye 
health. Regular exercise and maintaining a healthy weight also contribute to overall 
well-being, including eye health.

Avoiding Smoking

Smoking has been linked to an increased risk of developing age-related macular 
degeneration (AMD), cataracts, and other eye conditions. Quitting smoking or avoiding 
exposure to secondhand smoke is beneficial for both your general health and eye health.

Protecting Your Eyes from the Sun

Prolonged exposure to UV rays can lead to various eye conditions, including cataracts 
and macular degeneration. When outdoors, wear sunglasses that block 100% of UVA and 
UVB rays. Additionally, consider wearing a wide-brimmed hat for further protection.

Proper Contact Lens Care

If you wear contact lenses, it's crucial to follow the recommended cleaning and disinfection 
guidelines provided by your eye care professional. Improper contact lens hygiene can lead to 
eye infections and other complications.

Managing Eye Allergies

If you experience eye allergies, consult with an eye care professional to determine the
best course of action. They may recommend over-the-counter or prescription eye drops to 
alleviate symptoms and provide relief.

Regular Breaks from Digital Devices

Extended screen time can strain your eyes and contribute to digital eye strain. 
Taking regular breaks, adjusting display settings to reduce blue light emissions, 
and using artificial tears can help alleviate symptoms.
